Objective 1: Youth Co-Creation

Objective-level page for the youth co-creation workstream and its current supporting project references.

Objective

Co-create a shared understanding of the role of social determinants in young people’s mental health through a Youth Advisory Group.

Abstract

This deliverable captures the youth co-creation workstream that informs hypothesis generation, lay DAG development, interpretation, and dissemination across the CO-LUMINATE study. In the private rebuild, the work is organized in two linked phases:

  • a pre-secondary analysis phase that develops a youth-informed pathway inventory and conceptual framing before formal modeling
  • a post-secondary analysis phase that uses youth interpretation to contextualize findings and strengthen stakeholder-facing communication

Together, these phases ensure that the technical analytical sequence remains locally grounded without turning the co-creation workstream into a separate modeled dataset.

Keywords

youth co-creation; Youth Advisory Group; lay DAG; pathway inventory; interpretation workshop; dissemination

Pre-Secondary Analysis

The pre-secondary analysis phase establishes the interpretive foundations for the later quantitative work. Following an inception workshop, Youth Advisory Group members contribute photo diaries, group-based narrative building, and a co-developed lay DAG describing how everyday social stressors may shape emotional distress among young people in the AHRI surveillance setting.

This phase contributes a contextual pathway inventory rather than a separate statistical dataset. The inventory identifies locally salient domains that help explain how structural disadvantage may become embodied as distress, including food strain, unsafe home and community environments, substance-related risk, school adversity, weak social connectedness, family separation, service-access barriers, and spiritual or cultural support.

That inventory then provides contextual grounding for the later behavioral mediator analysis. It helps justify why certain adolescent pathway variables were prioritized, clarifies what those measured variables do and do not capture, and keeps interpretation anchored in adolescents’ own accounts of how disadvantage becomes distress.

In practical terms, the co-created pathway inventory strengthens the later pathway-screening work in two ways. First, it supports the selection of policy-relevant pathway domains that can be tested in the harmonized dataset. Second, it documents the broader pathway ecology that remains outside the measured variables, which is important for interpreting later findings with appropriate restraint.

Secondary Data Constraints

The inventory also makes an important secondary-data limitation visible. Even after careful harmonization, the measured adolescent pathway block captures only part of the broader pathway ecology described by young people. Several locally salient domains, including housing and water insecurity, migration-related family separation, service-access barriers, and spiritual or cultural support, are not directly measured in the current harmonized dataset.

This matters because the available indicators were assembled retrospectively across different source cohorts rather than designed as a single shared pathway instrument from the outset. As a result, a variable labelled as food insecurity, social support, school disengagement, or violence exposure may preserve the broad substantive domain while still differing in wording, response structure, timeframe, or local meaning across contributing studies.

The co-created pathway inventory therefore serves two purposes at once: it helps identify which measured pathway domains are locally grounded enough to carry forward into later analysis, and it clarifies where the secondary-data platform remains incomplete. This is why the measured pathway block should be understood as a constrained but policy-relevant subset of a wider adolescent pathway system, not as an exhaustive map of all relevant mechanisms.

Post-Secondary Analysis

The post-secondary analysis phase begins after the main quantitative results are available. In this phase, Youth Advisory Group members engage with the emerging findings through structured interpretation and reflection workshops.

This workstream is intended to:

  • refine the explanatory narrative around the quantitative findings
  • identify where technical results align with, simplify, or miss lived pathway experience
  • support dissemination materials that are legible to community and stakeholder audiences
  • maintain context-sensitive framing when the study moves from analysis outputs to interpretation and communication

This post-secondary phase does not replace the statistical analyses. Instead, it strengthens the interpretive bridge between the technical results and the broader community-facing meaning of those results.
