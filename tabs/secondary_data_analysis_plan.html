Statistical Analysis Plan

Overview

This document is the integrated CO-LUMINATE Statistical Analysis Plan (SAP). It retains the published public section structure while updating the underlying analytical description to reflect the current harmonization, trajectory, and pathway-screening workflows carried forward into the release-safe script bundles.

The current release includes:

  • Section A: Objective 2 (Depression Harmonization)
  • Section B: Objective 3 (Social Determinant Trajectories)
  • Section C: Objective 4 (Behavioral Mediators)

Section D (Objective 5: Causal Mediation) remains an intentional placeholder pending finalization of prespecified mediation model families and implementation diagnostics.

Section A: Objective 2 - Depression Harmonization SAP

A1 Background and Rationale

This SAP component defines the psychometric protocol for harmonizing depression measurement between the Shona Symptom Questionnaire and the Patient Health Questionnaire-9. The central challenge is not simple score conversion, but defensible alignment of instruments that differ in item format, recall period, and cultural grounding in a longitudinal multi-cohort setting.

The PHQ-9 is treated as the external benchmark for probable depression (Kroenke, Spitzer, and Williams 2001), while the SSQ contributes culturally grounded distress content that is relevant in Southern African settings (Patel et al. 1997; Haney et al. 2014; Chibanda et al. 2019). The revised Objective 2 workflow therefore treats harmonization as a staged psychometric program: item-set refinement, structural validation, invariance assessment, joint IRT calibration, and threshold comparison. In the current implementation, the theoretically derived SSQ-10 subset is retained as the main transportable SSQ configuration because it preserves the depressive core while avoiding non-specific distress items that weaken comparability with the PHQ benchmark (Putnick and Bornstein 2016; Woods 2009).

A3 Objectives and Estimands

Let \(Y_i^{PHQ} = \mathbb{1}\{PHQ9_i \ge 10\}\) denote the PHQ-referenced probable-depression indicator for participant \(i\).

The primary estimand is the harmonized binary endpoint

\[ Y_i^{H} = \mathbb{1}\{\hat{\theta}_i \ge \tau^\ast\}, \]

where \(\hat{\theta}_i\) is the calibrated latent depression score from joint PHQ-SSQ modeling and \(\tau^\ast\) is the selected latent threshold linked to the PHQ-9 reference scale.

Secondary estimands include prevalence alignment and threshold transportability:

\[ \Delta_p = \Pr(Y_i^{H}=1) - \Pr(Y_i^{PHQ}=1), \]

where \(\Delta_p\) summarizes endpoint-prevalence drift under candidate thresholding strategies. In the revised Objective 2 workflow, threshold comparison is not limited to a single rule. The anchored threshold linked to a PHQ-9 score of approximately 10 is compared with ROC-derived operating points and sensitivity- or specificity-targeted alternatives before a transport rule is selected.

A4 Study Design and Analytic Samples

The development sample comprises participants aged approximately 16-24 years with concurrent SSQ and PHQ observations available for joint calibration. The transport sample consists of SSQ-only records used to extend the harmonized scoring rule beyond the concurrent benchmark subset.

Eligibility is stage-specific:

  • structural-validity stages require complete item vectors for the candidate item set under evaluation;
  • IRT calibration requires valid item responses under the retained instrument configuration;
  • threshold comparison requires benchmark PHQ information;
  • endpoint assignment requires valid latent-score estimates from the final joint or fixed-parameter SSQ scoring workflow.

A5 Structural Modeling Strategy

The revised Objective 2 workflow evaluates three candidate SSQ configurations: SSQ-14, the theoretical SSQ-10 depressive subset, and the SSQ-8 comparison subset (Chibanda et al. 2019). Structural validity begins with Monte Carlo split-sample EFA using polychoric correlation structures and categorical-data retention criteria, followed by hold-out CFA using WLSMV estimation (Holgado–Tello et al. 2010; Wu and Estabrook 2016). Consensus item structures are identified across repeated split samples rather than from a single exploratory fit.

Let \(\mathbf{x}_i\) denote the ordinal or binary symptom vector and \(\theta_i\) the latent depression factor. The CFA measurement model is

\[ \mathbf{x}_i \sim f(\theta_i; \boldsymbol{\lambda}, \boldsymbol{\tau}), \]

where \(\boldsymbol{\lambda}\) are factor-loading parameters and \(\boldsymbol{\tau}\) are item-threshold parameters. Fit diagnostics are interpreted jointly rather than through single-cutoff rules, consistent with the revised psychometric workflow (Hu and Bentler 1999; MacCallum et al. 2002). Measurement invariance is then assessed across sex and age strata so that downstream thresholding does not rest on item structures that vary materially across key subgroups (Putnick and Bornstein 2016; Vandenberg and Lance 2000).

A6 IRT Harmonization and Threshold Derivation

Harmonization uses graded response models implemented in mirt (Samejima 1969; Chalmers 2012). For item \(j\) and category threshold \(k\),

\[ \Pr(X_{ij} \ge k \mid \theta_i) = \operatorname{logit}^{-1}\left[a_j(\theta_i - b_{jk})\right]. \]

Separate instrument-level GRMs are used to confirm single-scale adequacy, after which a joint PHQ-SSQ calibration places both instruments on a common latent continuum. Latent trait scores are estimated using expected a posteriori scoring, and SSQ parameters from the joint model are exported as a fixed-parameter scoring engine so that SSQ-only records can be scored without refitting the full calibration model.

The primary applied threshold remains a clinically anchored \(\theta\) cutoff tied to the PHQ-9 test characteristic curve at approximately 10. However, the revised Objective 2 work compares this anchored rule with Youden-based ROC rules and constrained operating rules targeting high sensitivity or specificity before selecting the transportable endpoint definition for downstream Objectives 3-5.

A7 Performance Metrics and Uncertainty

Endpoint performance is summarized by:

  • sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NPV, and accuracy;
  • AUC and Cohen’s \(\kappa\);
  • prevalence drift \(\Delta_p\);
  • subgroup consistency under alternate threshold strategies.

The current workflow treats discrimination, calibration, and prevalence alignment as a joint decision problem rather than selecting thresholds on AUC or Youden performance alone. Uncertainty is summarized using resampling-based intervals and repeated-split diagnostics where appropriate (Efron and Tibshirani 1994).

A8 Planned Outputs and Reporting

Planned outputs are:

  1. item-retention and structural-validity diagnostics for candidate SSQ configurations;
  2. CFA, invariance, and DIF summaries for the retained SSQ-based depression structure;
  3. joint IRT calibration outputs and threshold-mapping comparisons;
  4. a fixed-parameter SSQ scoring engine for SSQ-only cohorts;
  5. the final harmonized depression endpoint carried forward into downstream Objectives 3-5.

Section B: Objective 3 - Social Determinant Trajectories SAP

B1 Background and Rationale

Objective 3 models developmental trajectories of childhood social determinants and evaluates how those trajectories relate to the harmonized depression endpoint. This SAP treats trajectory modeling as a defensibility problem in which class structure, overlap, and interpretability will be examined across multiple analytic restrictions rather than inferred from a single retained solution (Andruff et al. 2009; D. S. Nagin 2005; Bengt O. Muthén 2001).

This framing follows the current harmonized trajectory workflow, which treats childhood social determinants as time-varying developmental processes and organizes them into early-childhood (0-5 years) and middle-late-childhood (6-12 years) windows (Hall and Posel 2019; Isabel Fortier et al. 2017; Simelane et al. 2025). The scientific aim is to preserve developmental timing while distinguishing robust trajectory structures from those that change materially when stronger repeated-measures requirements are imposed.

B3 Objectives and Estimands

Let \(E_{it}\) denote exposure profile at developmental time \(t\), and let \(C_i\) denote latent trajectory class membership.

Primary estimands include the class-proportion vector

\[ \pi_c = \Pr(C_i = c), \quad c = 1,\dots,K, \]

and class-conditional depression-risk contrasts

\[ \text{OR}_c = \frac{\Pr(Y_i^H=1 \mid C_i=c)/\Pr(Y_i^H=0 \mid C_i=c)} {\Pr(Y_i^H=1 \mid C_i=c_{ref})/\Pr(Y_i^H=0 \mid C_i=c_{ref})}. \]

Secondary estimands include posterior classification quality, same-label retention across analytic branches, and cross-scenario reallocation of class membership. These secondary estimands are defined because the Objective 3 workflow will compare unrestricted, primary restricted, sensitivity, and strict joint branches rather than relying on a single fitted branch as the sole evidence source.

B4 Exposure Definitions and Time Windows

Exposure histories are organized into two pre-specified developmental windows: 0-5 years and 6-12 years. All trajectory models are built on harmonized repeated childhood measures, with emphasis on preserving developmental ordering and avoiding post hoc redefinition of windows or determinant families.

The current active Objective 3 model family set is:

  • ULWOM: univariate maternal co-residence trajectories;
  • UHSES: univariate household socioeconomic status trajectories;
  • JPA: joint parental residence pattern trajectories;
  • JMAES: joint maternal absence plus household socioeconomic status trajectories.

These four families supersede the earlier, narrower public summary and define the current SAP implementation target. Retired or exploratory specifications are excluded from active SAP compliance checks unless they are reintroduced explicitly in a later release.

B5 LCGA and Joint-Trajectory Strategy

The Objective 3 workflow implements a five-branch comparison framework:

  1. unrestricted univariate analyses to maximize sample size under likelihood-based handling of incomplete repeated measures;
  2. primary univariate analyses requiring at least three occasions per window;
  3. sensitivity univariate analyses requiring at least two occasions per window;
  4. main joint analyses requiring at least three occasions;
  5. strict joint sensitivity analyses requiring at least three complete joint occasions.

For participant \(i\) in class \(c\), the class-specific growth model is

\[ E_{it} = \eta_{0c} + \eta_{1c}t + \eta_{2c}t^2 + \varepsilon_{it}, \quad \varepsilon_{it} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\sigma_c^2). \]

Class-membership probabilities are represented as

\[ \Pr(C_i=c) = \frac{\exp(\alpha_c)}{\sum_{h=1}^{K}\exp(\alpha_h)}. \]

In practice, the workflow emphasizes common orchestration for model fitting, labeling, plotting, reporting, and audit generation rather than isolated solution fitting (Bengt O. Muthén 2004; Daniel S. Nagin et al. 2016).

B6 Class Enumeration and Model Selection

Class enumeration combines statistical and scientific criteria:

  • information criteria and neighboring class-count stability;
  • minimum useful class size and posterior separation;
  • substantive interpretability of class shapes;
  • consistency of retained solutions across analytic restrictions;
  • documentation against GRoLTS and SMART-LCA-aligned reporting standards (Nylund, Asparouhov, and Muthén 2007; Masyn 2013; Lissa, Garnier-Villarreal, and Anadria 2024).

Because mixture solutions are sensitive to specification, local maxima, and sample restriction, retained solutions are also evaluated under structural sensitivity checks and overlap/reallocation reviews (Bauer and Curran 2003; Bauer 2007; Hipp and Bauer 2006; Watson et al. 2022).

B7 Association Modeling and Robustness

Downstream association models condition on the harmonized endpoint \(Y_i^H\) and fixed covariates once selected class structures are carried forward:

\[ \operatorname{logit}\Pr(Y_i^H=1) = \beta_0 + \sum_{c \ne c_{ref}}\beta_c\mathbb{1}(C_i=c) + \boldsymbol{\gamma}^\top\mathbf{Z}_i. \]

For this SAP, robustness is defined more broadly than the final regression stage alone. Planned robustness evidence includes:

  • class-count stability across unrestricted and restricted branches;
  • entropy and posterior-probability comparisons;
  • same-label retention in overlap samples;
  • strict-joint boundary-condition checks;
  • complete audit artifact coverage across all active branches.

This means the SAP treats defensibility and class reallocation as part of the inferential workflow rather than as a post hoc appendix to a single retained model (Schafer and Graham 2002; Graham 2009; Parsons, McCormick, et al. 2024).

B8 Planned Outputs and Reporting

Planned outputs include:

  1. trajectory-profile plots for each active model family and developmental window;
  2. class-enumeration summaries and posterior-quality diagnostics;
  3. overlap and reallocation summaries across unrestricted and restricted branches;
  4. structural-sensitivity and reporting-checklist artifacts;
  5. downstream class-linked depression association tables using the harmonized endpoint.

Section C: Objective 4 - Behavioral Mediators SAP

C1 Background and Rationale

Objective 4 identifies adolescent behavioral and proximal social pathway variables through which childhood social determinant trajectories may influence depression risk. This SAP defines Objective 4 as a pathway-screening and prioritization analysis, not a formal causal mediation decomposition. This distinction matters in a harmonized multi-cohort platform where pathway variables, endpoint timing, and contextual exposure history all vary across source studies (Jörns-Presentati et al. 2021; Jakobsson et al. 2024; Rossouw et al. 2024).

The current Objective 4 framing treats the adolescent pathway block as a constrained but policy-relevant subset of a broader locally grounded pathway ecology. Candidate domains include material deprivation, violence, school-related adversity, social connectedness, and sexual-risk behavior, all positioned after childhood exposure windows and before the harmonized depressive-symptom endpoint (M. Mkhize, Westhuizen, and Sorsdahl 2024; Partap et al. 2023; Zimmerman et al. 2021).

C3 Objectives and Estimands

Primary estimands are adjusted pathway-depression associations and pathway-prioritization signals derived from the joint pattern of upstream A-path support and downstream B-path evidence.

For mediator \(M_j\), the endpoint model is

\[ \operatorname{logit}\left\{P(Y_i^H=1 \mid S_{0-5}, S_{6-12}, M_1,\ldots,M_7, C)\right\} = \beta_0 + \beta_1^{\top}S_{0-5} + \beta_2^{\top}S_{6-12} + \sum_{j=1}^{7}\theta_j M_j + \beta_3^{\top} C, \]

where \(S_{0-5}\) and \(S_{6-12}\) denote childhood trajectory-class representations, \(M_j\) denotes a candidate adolescent pathway domain, and \(C\) denotes fixed covariates. The primary parameter of interest for each pathway domain is \(\theta_j\), interpreted as the pathway-specific association with the harmonized depression endpoint after conditioning on transition history and the fixed covariate set.

Secondary estimands include A-path support hierarchy, nested-adjustment attenuation patterns, and ranking stability across model families. These secondary estimands will be used to decide which pathway domains warrant promotion into later mediation-focused analyses.

C4 Mediator Definitions and Coding

The current empirically testable adolescent pathway block comprises seven harmonized domains:

  • alcohol use;
  • ever having had sexual intercourse;
  • food insecurity;
  • lack of government grant receipt;
  • violence exposure;
  • low social support;
  • school disengagement.

These variables are treated as candidate pathway indicators rather than as already-identified formal mediators. They are harmonized from adolescent data collected after the childhood exposure windows and before the harmonized depression endpoint, with fixed covariate adjustment anchored on sex, age, rural residence, and calendar year (Marlow et al. 2023; I. Fortier et al. 2017; Chilver et al. 2023; Mthiyane et al. 2025).

C5 Modeling Strategy

Objective 4 carries forward transition-based exposure history rather than using isolated class snapshots. The childhood transition backbone is written as

\[ P(S_{0-5}, S_{6-12}, U^{(0-5)}, U^{(6-12)}) = P(S_{0-5}) \times P(S_{6-12} \mid S_{0-5}) \times \prod_t P(U_t^{(0-5)} \mid S_{0-5}) \times \prod_t P(U_t^{(6-12)} \mid S_{6-12}), \]

with posterior class uncertainty carried into downstream screening rather than resolved through deterministic single-class assignment (Vermunt 2010; Asparouhov and Muthén 2014; Zyberaj, Bakaç, and Seibel 2022).

Within that transition framework, three analytic layers are prespecified:

  1. A-path screening of pathway-variable probabilities as functions of childhood transition history and fixed covariates;
  2. B-path models relating pathway domains to the harmonized depression endpoint after adjustment for transition history and fixed covariates;
  3. jointly adjusted pathway models used to rank candidate pathway domains rather than to claim identified indirect effects.

All downstream regression stages use posterior-probability-weighted logistic models with participant-level cluster-robust standard errors. The active model-family set is aligned to Objective 3: ULWOM, UHSES, JPA, and JMAES.

C6 Mediator Prioritization and Phenotype Planning

Pathway prioritization for Objective 5 will be based on the joint pattern of:

  • A-path support across pre-specified primary and secondary hypothesis tiers;
  • B-path stability across the four active model families;
  • attenuation under nested fixed-covariate adjustment;
  • substantive plausibility in relation to the broader co-created pathway ecology.

Within that framework, food insecurity, violence exposure, and ever having had sexual intercourse are treated as high-interest candidate domains for close scrutiny because they are strongly motivated by the current substantive framework and the pathway-screening design. Alcohol use, lack of government grant receipt, low social support, and school disengagement remain part of the full evaluated pathway block and will be assessed under the same prioritization rules rather than excluded a priori (Duby et al. 2022; S. Mkhize et al. 2022).

C7 Sensitivity and Robustness

Objective 4 places particular emphasis on nested fixed-covariate adjustment. Sensitivity analyses will assess whether attenuation of pathway-depression associations is driven primarily by age, calendar year, rural residence, or combinations of these factors. This reflects the design assumption that developmental timing and secular period are part of the data-generating structure rather than optional nuisance adjustments.

Additional robustness layers include:

  • pathway ranking by pre-specified primary and secondary A-path hypothesis tiers rather than nominal significance alone (Benjamini and Hochberg 1995);
  • complete-case main analyses with targeted multiple-imputation diagnostics for maternal baseline variables (White, Royston, and Wood 2011; Sterne et al. 2009);
  • interaction reviews for selected pathway combinations, especially violence exposure with school disengagement;
  • reporting aligned to STROBE and AGReMA guidance (Elm et al. 2007; Moreno-Betancur et al. 2021).

These robustness checks reinforce that the current objective is prioritization for later mediation work, not a claim of identified direct and indirect effects (Imai, Keele, and Yamamoto 2010; Valeri and VanderWeele 2013; T. J. VanderWeele et al. 2016).

C8 Planned Outputs and Reporting

Planned outputs include:

  1. A-path hierarchy tables by hypothesis tier and model family;
  2. B-path association tables for the seven pathway domains;
  3. nested-adjustment attenuation reviews showing the effect of age, rural residence, and calendar year;
  4. a transparent shortlist of pathway domains prioritized for Objective 5;
  5. contextual summaries linking the measured pathway block to the broader co-created pathway inventory.

Supplementary: Glossary of Symbols and Parameters

This glossary consolidates the core notation used in Sections A-C. Equation-specific parameter definitions remain embedded in the relevant sections.

Objective 2 Glossary

  • \(i\): participant index.
  • \(j\): item index.
  • \(k\): ordered-category threshold index for item \(j\).
  • \(Y_i^{PHQ}\): PHQ-referenced probable-depression indicator.
  • \(Y_i^H\): harmonized depression indicator.
  • \(\hat{\theta}_i\): participant-specific estimated latent depression score.
  • \(\tau^\ast\): selected latent threshold used to classify \(Y_i^H\).
  • \(\Delta_p\): prevalence drift between harmonized and PHQ-referenced endpoint prevalence.
  • \(\mathbf{x}_i\): vector of observed symptom item responses.
  • \(\boldsymbol{\lambda}\): factor-loading parameters.
  • \(\boldsymbol{\tau}\): CFA item-threshold parameters.
  • \(a_j\): GRM discrimination parameter for item \(j\).
  • \(b_{jk}\): GRM category-threshold parameter for item \(j\) at threshold \(k\).

Objective 3 Glossary

  • \(i\): participant index.
  • \(t\): developmental-time index within a childhood window.
  • \(c, h\): latent-class indices.
  • \(K\): total number of retained or candidate classes.
  • \(E_{it}\): observed exposure value for participant \(i\) at time \(t\).
  • \(C_i\): latent trajectory class membership.
  • \(\pi_c\): marginal class proportion for class \(c\).
  • \(Y_i^H\): harmonized depression outcome from Objective 2.
  • \(c_{ref}\): reference class for class-contrast models.
  • \(\eta_{0c}, \eta_{1c}, \eta_{2c}\): class-specific intercept and slope parameters.
  • \(\varepsilon_{it}\): residual term for participant \(i\) at time \(t\).
  • \(\sigma_c^2\): class-specific residual variance.
  • \(\alpha_c\): multinomial logit intercept governing class-membership probability.
  • \(\beta_c\): class-effect coefficient for class \(c\) relative to \(c_{ref}\).

Objective 4 Glossary

  • \(S_{0-5}\): childhood trajectory representation for the 0-5 year window.
  • \(S_{6-12}\): childhood trajectory representation for the 6-12 year window.
  • \(U^{(0-5)}, U^{(6-12)}\): observed childhood indicators contributing to each latent transition block.
  • \(M_j\): candidate adolescent pathway variable \(j\).
  • \(Y_i^H\): harmonized depression outcome.
  • \(C\): fixed covariate set used in the pathway-screening models.
  • \(\theta_j\): pathway-specific association parameter for mediator \(j\).
  • \(\beta_1, \beta_2\): transition-history effect vectors for the two childhood windows.
  • \(\beta_3\): fixed-covariate effect vector.
